## SEO & Content Strategy

**Organic strength**: ~8.8M–10M+ visits/month across multiple 2025 readings — the largest of the three competitors profiled here by a clear margin.

**⚠️ Material, recent, disclosed weakness**: Info Edge's own Q4 FY26 earnings call (reported by Medianama, May 2026) states that AI-driven changes in Google search behavior — i.e., the AI Overviews zero-click effect — have been "choking" Shiksha's referral search traffic for **several consecutive quarters**, with a direct, quantified billing/revenue hit disclosed this quarter. This is not analyst speculation; it's the parent company's own stated explanation for underperformance, made to investors.

This is the single most actionable data point in this round of research: Shiksha's core traffic-acquisition channel (organic Google search) is structurally declining due to AI Overviews intercepting queries before users click through. Any platform that wants to win the queries Shiksha used to own should optimize explicitly for AI Overview citation (GEO) rather than only classic blue-link SEO — because Shiksha, the market leader by traffic, is demonstrably losing exactly that battle right now.

## Competitive Implications for CollegeDelhi.com

**Where they're strong vs. us**: Raw traffic scale, Info Edge brand trust, breadth of national exam/course coverage.

**Where we're strong vs. them**: Shiksha's stated AI Overview problem is a direct opening — collegedelhi.com's pages are already built with the GEO fundamentals Shiksha appears to be losing on: FAQ-rich, self-contained 134–167-word-ish answer blocks, specific cited statistics (fees, placement LPA, NAAC grades, CAT cutoffs), and three JSON-LD schemas per page (FAQ, BreadcrumbList, ItemList) server-rendered at build time. If Google's AI Overviews are increasingly answering "which MBA college in Delhi" queries directly, the source they cite needs to be exactly this kind of structured, fact-dense, locally-specific content — which is precisely what's been built across the 40+ Delhi MBA/engineering pages this session.

**Opportunities**: Shiksha's disclosed weakness is industry-wide (it affects the whole organic-search-dependent category, including collegedunia and us), but Shiksha said it out loud to investors — meaning it's likely *more* exposed than competitors because more of its volume historically came from generic, broad-match informational queries that AI Overviews now answer directly. Locally-specific, comparison-heavy queries ("FMS vs IIFT," "MBA fees Delhi NCR 2026") are harder for a generic AI Overview to fully satisfy without citing a source — which favors a specific, well-structured site over a broad database.

**Threats**: Info Edge has deep pockets and could fund a GEO-optimization push to recover lost ground, which would directly compete with the same long-tail Delhi queries we're targeting.
