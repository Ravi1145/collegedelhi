## Landscape Overview

This is a mature, three-tier Indian edtech/admissions-discovery market. Shiksha (Info Edge-owned,
launched 2008) and Collegedunia (bootstrapped, launched 2013) are the established incumbents,
both running a near-identical playbook: free exam/college predictor tools as the traffic wedge,
monetized via lead-generation to colleges and counselling services. KollegeApply (founded March
2025 by a Collegedunia alumnus) is a much earlier-stage, angel-funded challenger explicitly
positioning around "AI-powered" discovery rather than the legacy predictor-tool model. The most
consequential finding in this round of research is not competitive in the usual sense — it's
structural: Info Edge's own Q4 FY26 earnings disclosure states that AI Overviews are actively
"choking" Shiksha's organic search traffic, a problem that almost certainly affects the entire
category, including Collegedunia and collegedelhi.com, but which the market leader has now
confirmed out loud to investors.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The category's biggest competitor right now isn't a company — it's Google AI Overviews.**
   Shiksha's own parent company has disclosed, in an earnings call, that this is actively reducing
   its organic traffic. Any platform optimizing specifically for AI Overview citation (not just
   classic blue-link ranking) has a real near-term opening against the market leader.

2. **Collegedunia and Shiksha run the same playbook** — predictor tools + discovery database +
   lead-gen monetization — and compete mostly on scale and brand rather than product
   differentiation. Neither appears (from this research) to be deeply investing in geography-
   specific content depth the way collegedelhi.com's MBA-Delhi build-out does.

3. **KollegeApply is the most vulnerable competitor to outrank on long-tail, Delhi-specific terms**
   right now — it's 14 months old, angel-seeded only, and has a smaller claimed institutional
   database than the incumbents. It is not yet a serious near-term threat, but its founder's
   insider knowledge of Collegedunia's model means it could move faster than its funding stage
   suggests once it finds traction. Worth re-profiling in 3–6 months.

4. **No competitor in this set appears to specialize in Delhi NCR specifically.** All three are
   national-scope generalists. Collegedelhi.com's entire content strategy this session — 40+
   pages with exact per-college fee/placement/cutoff/NAAC data, FAQ schema, comparison pages
   (FMS vs IIFT, DTU vs NSUT) — is structurally differentiated from anything found in this
   research, not just incrementally better.

5. **This research has real gaps that should be closed before treating it as decision-grade**:
   no backlink/domain-authority data, no precise organic-keyword-overlap data, and no verified
   homepage/pricing copy for any of the three (all direct scrapes were blocked). A follow-up pass
   with Firecrawl + DataForSEO MCP tools connected would substantially upgrade confidence,
   particularly on the "where do we have a content gap to exploit" question.

## Gaps and Opportunities

- **AI Overview / GEO citation gap**: Shiksha's disclosed weakness is the single most actionable
  finding here. Worth explicitly auditing collegedelhi.com's top Delhi MBA/engineering pages
  against Google's AI Overview citation patterns (the `seo-geo` skill's citability scoring) to
  confirm we're positioned to capture queries Shiksha is losing.
- **Regional depth gap**: none of the three profiled competitors appear to run dedicated,
  fact-dense Delhi-NCR-specific content at the page count and specificity collegedelhi.com now has.
- **KollegeApply re-check**: schedule a follow-up profile in Q4 2026 once it has measurable
  traffic/backlink data — it's the one competitor whose trajectory could change fastest.
- **Data gap to close next**: connect Firecrawl + DataForSEO MCP tools for a true deep profile —
  current profiles are WebSearch-only and explicitly flagged as lower-confidence on
  positioning/messaging claims.
